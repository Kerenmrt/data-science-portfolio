# Data Wrangling Analysis of Civil Servants in Southern Sumatra

## Overview

This project presents the application of **data wrangling techniques** to analyze the distribution of Civil Servants (PNS) in Southern Sumatra in 2021. The analysis focuses on transforming raw administrative datasets into structured and analyzable data to generate meaningful statistical insights.

The dataset initially contained several inconsistencies such as missing values, duplicate records, and inconsistent formats. Therefore, a series of data wrangling processes were applied to clean, transform, and restructure the data before performing statistical analysis and visualization.

---

## Objectives

The objectives of this project include:

- Applying multiple **data wrangling techniques** to improve dataset quality
- Creating new analytical variables for deeper analysis
- Performing **data cleaning and transformation**
- Integrating datasets from different sources
- Reshaping data structure for statistical analysis
- Producing insights related to the distribution of civil servants

---

## Data Wrangling Process

Several data wrangling techniques were implemented in this project:

1. **Dataset Input**
2. **Data Structure Inspection**
3. **Variable Creation and Subsetting**
4. **Data Sorting**
5. **Data Recoding**
6. **Data Merging**
7. **Data Reshaping**

These steps ensure that the dataset becomes clean, structured, and ready for statistical analysis.

---

## Key Findings

From the analysis of the civil servant dataset in Southern Sumatra:

- The total number of civil servants recorded was **12,729 employees across 41 institutions**.
- **Female employees accounted for 59%**, while **male employees accounted for 41%**.
- The largest institutions in terms of employee numbers include:
  - **Department of Education:** 7,775 employees
  - **Department of Health:** 810 employees
  - **Regional Revenue Agency:** 418 employees

The results highlight workforce distribution patterns across government institutions and gender composition in public sector employment.
